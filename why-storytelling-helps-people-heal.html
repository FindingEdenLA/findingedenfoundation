Why Storytelling Helps People Heal

There is something that changes when people finally tell the truth about what they have lived through. Not the cleaned-up version. Not the one designed to make everyone else comfortable. The real one.

Storytelling matters because pain gets distorted in isolation. People start minimizing what happened, doubting themselves, or turning their experiences into something smaller and easier to survive. Putting words to a life experience can change the relationship someone has to it.

Being witnessed matters

A story told in private is meaningful. A story witnessed with care can be even more powerful. Many people have never had a space where their honesty was received without being corrected, rushed, or made more digestible for someone else.
